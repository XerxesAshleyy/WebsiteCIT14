About Us

Taiyaki (鯛焼き, lit. ‘baked sea bream’) is a Japanese fish-shaped cake. It imitates the shape of the tai (Japanese red seabream), which it is named after.

Taiyaki is made using regular pancake or waffle batter. The batter is poured into a fish-shaped mold for each side. The filling is then put on one side and the mold is closed. It is then cooked on both sides until golden brown.

Because it was thought to be lucky, charms and decorations in the shape of the sea bream became popular. This even extended to tai shaped food such as taiyaki. It is thought to have originated from a similar sweet called imagawayaki. Imagawayaki is made of the same ingredients and in the same process as taiyaki.
